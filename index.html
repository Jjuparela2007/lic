The Mattes Method

Active Isolated Stretching.

A systematic method of muscle lengthening and fascial release that redefines what it means to stretch. Trained directly by Aaron Mattes, the developer of the method.

/ 01

Restore range of motion

Active movement balances the pull of soft tissue across a joint, restoring function to both superficial and deep fascial planes.

/ 02

Decrease pain & build strength

AIS helps decrease pain, improve joint flexibility and balance, expand range of motion, and increase muscle strength.

/ 03

Prevent injury, aid recovery

It can assist in injury prevention, improve athletic performance, and aid recovery after a workout.

Unlike prolonged static stretching — long held the gold standard — which can decrease blood flow and cause irritation, AIS works with the body's physiology rather than against it. Learn more about Aaron Mattes, the definitive educator on flexibility, at stretchingusa.com.

I'm a New York State licensed massage therapist specializing in therapeutic massage and Active Isolated Stretching. I earned an Associates Degree in Occupational Studies in 2003 from the Swedish Institute College of Health Sciences, one of the oldest massage schools in the country, and was a massage therapist at The Yale Club of New York in Manhattan, where I met clients from all over the world.

My practice has allowed me to work with all sorts of people — mothers, seniors, artists, casual athletes to ultra-marathoners, and performers and dancers at the Mark Morris Dance Center. I've learned directly from industry experts including Aaron Mattes, Mark Allen, and Shari Auth, LMT.

Mobile reflexology

Mobile Reflexology

The application of appropriate pressure by thumbs and fingers to specific points on the hands and feet to improve health — conveniently done in a special reflexology lounge chair. These reflex points correspond to different body organs and systems, and studies link this therapy to reduced sick leave and absenteeism.
